    <h2>Where I come from</h2>
    <p>My current path is built around healthcare, but it did not begin as a single-track professional story. The consistent thread has been communication: understanding how people explain themselves, how systems become difficult to enter, and how language can either clarify or block action.</p>

    <h2>Communication before healthcare</h2>
    <p>Public speaking, hosting, bilingual communication, and media-facing work shaped the way I think. They trained me to read audiences, structure complex messages, and move between cultural contexts without assuming everyone shares the same references.</p>

    <h2>Why nursing matters</h2>
    <p>Nursing gives that communication work a human and clinical grounding. It brings the questions closer to patients, families, older adults, care teams, health education, and the daily reality of decision-making under uncertainty.</p>

    <h2>How the other pieces connect</h2>
    <p>AI, entrepreneurship, social innovation, and interdisciplinary collaboration are directions I am actively exploring, not finished identities. They matter because healthcare problems are rarely only healthcare problems. They involve behaviour, trust, operations, education, culture, and systems design.</p>

    <h2>Problems I want to work on</h2>
    <p>I am especially interested in healthcare communication, ageing and cognitive health, AI literacy in health sciences education, public-facing health innovation, and interdisciplinary teams that can turn scattered expertise into practical work.</p>
