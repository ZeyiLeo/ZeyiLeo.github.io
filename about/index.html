About ZY LEE

A healthcare student learning how ideas move between people.

I am Leo, publicly presented as ZY LEE: a BSc Nursing student at The University of Hong Kong with work and interests crossing healthcare, communication, AI literacy, social innovation, entrepreneurship, and cross-cultural collaboration.

Where I come from

My current path is built around healthcare, but it did not begin as a single-track professional story. The consistent thread has been communication: understanding how people explain themselves, how systems become difficult to enter, and how language can either clarify or block action.

Communication before healthcare

Public speaking, hosting, bilingual communication, and media-facing work shaped the way I think. They trained me to read audiences, structure complex messages, and move between cultural contexts without assuming everyone shares the same references.

Why nursing matters

Nursing gives that communication work a human and clinical grounding. It brings the questions closer to patients, families, older adults, care teams, health education, and the daily reality of decision-making under uncertainty.

How the other pieces connect

AI, entrepreneurship, social innovation, and interdisciplinary collaboration are directions I am actively exploring, not finished identities. They matter because healthcare problems are rarely only healthcare problems. They involve behaviour, trust, operations, education, culture, and systems design.

Problems I want to work on

I am especially interested in healthcare communication, ageing and cognitive health, AI literacy in health sciences education, public-facing health innovation, and interdisciplinary teams that can turn scattered expertise into practical work.
